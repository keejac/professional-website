Background

I studied art education at St. Olaf College, training with practicing K-12 art educators in public and private schools in Minneapolis and across southern Minnesota. Through this program, I developed and implemented lessons using discipline-based arts education.

I studied interdisciplinary fine art at Stuart Weitzman School of Design and University of Pennsylvania, through which I earned a Certificate in Interactive and Time-Based Media, won the Susan C. Coslett Travelling Fellowship, and secured a graduate lecturer position at the Institute of Contemporary Art - Philadelphia.

I taught art and design classes at University of Pennsylvania; Kutztown University; Antoinette Westphal College of Media, Art, and Design at Drexel University; and Pennsylvania College of Art and Design. I guest lectured at Institute of Contemporary Art - Philadelphia; University of the Arts; and Tyler School of Art and Architecture at Temple University.

I built curricular products at MobyMax, an EdTech company. These products are used across 80% of public US schools. I developed core sections of MobyMax’s flagship math product as a curriculum developer, led art direction across all math products as math design lead, and co-created and ran the training program for new curriculum developers.

When the covid-19 pandemic began, I supported the digital transformation to remote learning for the Design Foundation at Antoinette Westphal School of Media, Art, and Design at Drexel University.
